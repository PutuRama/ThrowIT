What is the purpose of having a Trash Bank?

The primary purpose of a trash bank is to encourage the community to manage waste responsibly while keeping the environment clean. It aims to reduce the volume of trash sent to landfills by promoting recycling. Additionally, it provides economic benefits by turning household waste into a valuable source of extra income.

Do the payments include a fee?

Regarding payments, you typically have two convenient options. You can choose to deliver your sorted waste directly to the bank to receive the full payment without deductions. Alternatively, you can opt for a transport service to pick up the waste from your home, which usually involves a small service fee.

Can everyone become a customer of the Garbage Bank?

Yes, everyone is welcome to become a customer! Whether you are an individual, a household, a student, or even a small business, you can join. Most banks only require a simple registration process to open a "trash savings account," making it easy for the whole community to participate and save.

How is waste assessed at the Waste Bank?

Waste is assessed based on its specific category and its total weight. When you deposit your items, the staff will categorize them—such as plastic, paper, or metal—and weigh them carefully. The value is then calculated using the current market price for each material before being added to your account.
